## Budget Types

### Daily Budget

Spend up to a set amount per day.

- **Pros**: Consistent daily spend, easy to manage
- **Cons**: May not spend full budget on low-activity days
- **Best for**: Ongoing campaigns, steady presence

### Lifetime Budget

Spend a total amount over the campaign duration.

- **Pros**: Optimizes spend across best-performing days
- **Cons**: Spend can be uneven day-to-day
- **Best for**: Fixed-duration promotions, events

## Setting Your Budget

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose Budget Type">
    Select daily or lifetime based on your campaign needs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Amount">
    Set your budget amount in your currency.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Duration">
    For lifetime budgets, set start and end dates.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Multi-Platform Allocation

When launching to both Meta and Google:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set Total Budget">
    Enter your total campaign budget.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Allocate Percentage">
    Split budget between platforms (e.g., 70% Meta, 30% Google).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review Split">
    Aventor calculates the dollar amount for each platform.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Duration

- **Continuous**: Run until manually stopped
- **Fixed dates**: Set specific start and end dates
- **Event-based**: Common for promotions and launches

## Minimum Budgets

<Warning>
Minimum daily budget varies by platform:
- **Meta Ads**: Typically $1/day minimum per ad set
- **Google Ads**: Varies by campaign type

Aventor will warn you if your budget is below platform minimums.
</Warning>

## Budget Recommendations

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="New campaigns">
    Start with $20-50/day to gather data. Scale up based on performance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Testing creatives">
    Allocate equal budget across ad variations for fair comparison.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Scaling">
    Increase budget by 20-30% at a time to maintain performance.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Cost Factors

What affects your cost:
- **Audience size** - Smaller audiences = higher costs
- **Competition** - Popular audiences cost more
- **Creative quality** - Better ads = lower costs
- **Relevance score** - More relevant = cheaper delivery
